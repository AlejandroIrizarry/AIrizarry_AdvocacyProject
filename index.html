Climate Justice: Green Justice for all

Equal rights for everyone for a healthy environment for all.

What is Climate Justice?

Climate justice recognises the climate crisis as a social and political problem, as well as an environmental one. It acknowledges that different communities feel the effects of the climate crisis differently, and that the responsibility for the crisis rests with some countries and companies more than others. And it understands that the lives of those already facing injustice and oppression – like the LGBTQI+ community in Jamaica,  for example – are made harder by the impacts of the climate crisis (Friends of Earth, 2022).

How Climate Change affects communities

Due to Climate Change and the negative effects it brings to different communities, it is common knowledge that the people who do the least to contribute to climate change, are the ones who pay mostly for it. Due to the disproportionaly in our society, people from low-income and underrepresented communities are the ones who suffer the worst of its effects. The effects include flooding, rising sea levels, heatwaves, forest fires, drought, amonsgt other caused by the climate breakdown.

This breakdown impacts minority communities that include people of colour, LGBTQI+ people, women, disabled people, the working class and generally, people that are categorized as low-income individuals. These communities continue, to this day, to face the burdens of choices made by big companies, governments and elite classes in first-world countries that overexploit and consume the world's resources, which therefore make the climate crisis worse every single day.

The disproportionality caused by this climate breakdown that these communities have to face, due to lack of resources, is one of the greatest injustice of the climate crisis.

Importance of Climate Justice

Unfortunately, not every community faces the burdens of climate change the same way. This is due to the unfair distribution of resources that should also be accessible to other communities no matter their economic and social status. By enforcing the Climate Justice Movement, we are respecting and protecting human rights, supporting right to development, sharing benefits and burdens equitably, ensuring that decisions on climate change are participatory, transparent and accountable and the movement also highlights gender equality and equity.
